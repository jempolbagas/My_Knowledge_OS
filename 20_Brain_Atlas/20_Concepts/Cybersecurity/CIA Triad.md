## The idea (one clear statement)
The CIA Triad is the foundational framework of cybersecurity, postulating that information security must balance three core pillars: **Confidentiality** (preventing unauthorized access), **Integrity** (preventing unauthorized modification), and **Availability** (ensuring authorized access when needed).

## Why it matters / how it connects
Every security control, policy, or vulnerability remediation is designed to protect one or more of these three pillars:
*   **Confidentiality:** Handled via encryption, access controls (IAM), and multi-factor authentication.
*   **Integrity:** Handled via cryptographic hashing (e.g., SHA-256), digital signatures, and version controls.
*   **Availability:** Handled via redundancy, backups, load balancing, and DDoS mitigation.

A failure in any of these components compromises the entire security posture of a system.
