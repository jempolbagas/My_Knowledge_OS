# Astronomy Olympiad PKM

Welcome to your Astronomy Olympiad Personal Knowledge Management (PKM) vault.

## Directory Organization

This structure is designed to separate conceptual learning from practical problem-solving, while keeping track of your progress and resources.

- **`00_Meta/`**: Infrastructure for your vault. Store Obsidian templates, scripts, and tag indices here.
- **`10_Syllabus/`**: The roadmap. Keep the official IOAA/National Olympiad syllabus here, along with your study plans and progress trackers.
- **`20_Concepts/`**: Your core knowledge base. This is where you write notes, derive formulas, and summarize concepts. It's subdivided into key astronomical fields:
  - `01_Spherical_Astronomy/`: Celestial sphere, coordinates, time, seasons.
  - `02_Celestial_Mechanics/`: Kepler's laws, orbits, gravitation, N-body problems.
  - `03_Solar_System/`: Planets, moons, minor bodies, solar physics.
  - `04_Stellar_Astrophysics/`: Magnitudes, radiation, stellar structure, evolution, HR diagram.
  - `05_Galaxies_and_Cosmology/`: Milky Way, ISM, expanding universe, Big Bang.
  - `06_Observational_Techniques/`: Telescopes, detectors, spectroscopy, coordinate transformations.
- **`30_Problems/`**: The practice arena. Essential for Olympiad success.
  - `Past_Papers/`: Full exams from previous years (IOAA, APAO, National).
  - `Problem_Sets/`: Topical practice problems and your solutions.
- **`40_Resources/`**: Reference materials.
  - `Books/`: Notes from textbooks (e.g., Carroll & Ostlie, Karttunen).
  - `Formulas/`: Master formula sheets, astronomical constants, and cheat sheets.
- **`50_Study_Journal/`**: Your daily log. Track what you studied, log difficult problems you need to revisit, and plan your next sessions.

## Getting Started
1. Review the syllabus and start breaking it down into topics in `10_Syllabus`.
2. Begin taking notes in `20_Concepts`.
3. Consolidate formulas into `40_Resources/Formulas`.
4. Regularly practice problems from `30_Problems`.
